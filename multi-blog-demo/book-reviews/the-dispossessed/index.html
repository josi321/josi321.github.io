The Dispossessed, Ursula K. Le Guin

April 8, 2026

Read this on a flight back from a client visit and finished it the next morning at the kitchen table. It’s about a physicist defecting from a moon colony to the planet his ancestors fled, and it’s structured as alternating chapters, one timeline going forward, one going backward, meeting in the middle.

The structural trick is the part everyone praises and it works. But the thing that stuck with me was the politics. Le Guin makes both societies look livable and both look corrupting, and she does it without ever telling you which one she prefers. That’s harder than it sounds. Most political novels lose their nerve in the third act and pick a side. She just keeps showing you the trade-offs.

Worth the read if you’re tired of stories where the right answer is obvious.
