Hugo's per-section RSS is the multi-blog feature

April 15, 2026

Most static-site generators give you one RSS feed for the whole site. Hugo gives you one per section, and that turns out to be the whole game when you want to run multiple distinct blogs from one repo.

The relevant config:

outputs:
  home: [HTML, RSS]
  section: [HTML, RSS]

With that set, every directory under content/ becomes its own publication with its own feed. A reader who only cares about your tech notes can subscribe to /tech-notes/index.xml and never see your book reviews. The feeds carry independent metadata, so each “blog” can have its own author and description in the RSS envelope without leaking into the others.

The implication for content strategy: you can run 5 niche blogs as cleanly as you can run 1 generalist blog, and the SEO surface area scales with the number of niche topics, not with the size of any single blog. For a freelance content operation where each niche targets a different buyer persona, this is the cheapest multiplier in the stack.
